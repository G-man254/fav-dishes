PILAU MASALA

Our Pilau Masala is a flavorful and aromatic dish made with basmati majorly enjoyed and common in the swahili areas

  1. Basmati rice (2 cups)
  2. Chicken (2 thigh fillets)
  3. Beef (quarter kg)
  4. Onions (3 medium-sized)
  5. Tomatoes (3 finely chopped)
  6. Garlic (3 cloves)
  7. Cooking oil(3 tbsp)
  8. water (4 cups)
  9. salt (to taste)
  10. Spices
    • Cumin: 1 tspn
    • Coriander: 1 tspn (ground)
    • Cinnamon: 1 stick
    • Cardamom: 4 pods
    • Black pepper: 1 tspn (whole or freshly ground)

RECIPE

Follow these steps to make our Pilau Masala

Step 1: Prepare the Meat

  • In a large pot, heat 1 tbsp of cooking oil over medium heat.
  • Add the beef and brown it until it’s seared on all sides. Add a little water, cover, and simmer until tender (20–30 minutes for tender cuts, longer for tougher cuts).
  • Remove the beef from the pot and set it aside.
  • In the same pot, brown the chicken pieces lightly. Remove and set aside.

Step 2: Sauté the Base

  • Add the remaining 2 tbsp of oil to the pot.
  • Sauté the sliced onions until they are golden brown (this gives pilau its rich flavor).
  • Add the garlic and ginger paste, and cook for another minute until fragrant.

Step 3: Add the Spices

  • Add the garlic and ginger paste, and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  • Cook for 1–2 minutes to toast the spices and release their aroma.

Step 4: Add the Meat

  • Add the chopped tomatoes to the pot and cook until they soften and form a thick sauce.
  • Return the beef and chicken to the pot, mixing well with the sauce and spices.
  • Season with salt and cook for 5–7 minutes to let the flavors combine.

Step 5: Add the Rice

  • Add the rice to the pot and mix it thoroughly with the meat and spices.
  • Pour in the water or broth (use 4 cups for 2 cups of rice). Stir gently and bring to a boil.
  • Once it starts boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and simmer until the rice is cooked and the liquid is absorbed (about 20 minutes).
  • Check occasionally to ensure the rice doesn’t stick to the bottom.

Step 6: Serve

  • Once the rice is tender and fluffy, turn off the heat and let it sit covered for 5 minutes.
  • Fluff the rice gently with a fork to mix the meat evenly.
  • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves if desired and serve hot.
